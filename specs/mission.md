# Mission

## What We're Building

Trainlytics is a personal fitness operating system — a single, flexible tool for planning, logging, analyzing, and continuously improving training across all activity types.

## The Problem

Existing fitness apps force users into predefined workflows built around a single discipline:
- Running apps lack detailed strength tracking
- Gym apps ignore cardio metrics like pace or heart rate zones
- Planning and progress analysis are fragmented across multiple tools
- Sharing data for coaching or AI analysis is inconvenient

Hybrid athletes — people who run, lift, cycle, and mix modalities — are left stitching together multiple apps that don't talk to each other.

## Why We're Building It

Three things set Trainlytics apart, and all three are equally core to the mission:

**1. Hybrid training support**
One place to log cardio (duration, distance, pace, HR zones) and strength (exercises, sets, reps, weights, progression) without compromise. The app adapts to the training style, not the other way around.

**2. Data ownership, integrations, and AI coaching**
Users own their data and can export structured session summaries for coaching or external tools. Trainlytics also pulls data in: Strava activities sync automatically, and an Apple Health export brings in workouts, sleep, HRV, resting HR, and body weight — giving the app a complete picture of the athlete, not just the sessions they log manually.

On top of that data, Trainlytics integrates directly with the Claude API for in-app AI coaching: a weekly insights panel compares the current training week against the previous five; an adaptive session helper lets the user describe how they feel and receive concrete workout modifications; and an agentic plan editor can review the past week and propose specific changes to next week's plan — sessions to add, move, or remove — which the user approves one by one before anything is saved.

**3. Templates, planning, and reflection**
Reusable strength session templates so users never have to reconstruct a workout from memory. Smart defaults pre-fill exercise parameters from the last logged session, and exercise replacements let users swap movements in one tap. Pre-training wellbeing and post-session RPE are captured alongside every workout so athletes can track how readiness affects performance over time. A planning layer (built on top of templates) lets users build weekly training plans, compare planned vs. completed workouts, and iterate. Training is intentional, not just reactive.

## Who It's For

- Hybrid athletes combining cardio and strength training
- Data-driven fitness enthusiasts who want real insights, not surface-level stats
- Users experimenting with AI coaching and training optimization

## What Success Looks Like

A user can open the app and immediately feel at home — a polished, vibrant interface with the Trainlytics brand. They pick a strength template, adjust what changed that day, and commit the session in seconds. Their cardio run logs distance in km, pace in min/km, and a heart rate zone breakdown showing whether the effort was truly aerobic. History shows meaningful stats at a glance — volume, pace, avg HR — and a weekly summary card breaks down minutes and calories between cardio and strength.

Over time, the picture deepens. The Analytics tab shows how squat weight has progressed, where personal records sit, how cardio time splits across run vs. cycle vs. walk, and whether training consistency has improved over the past year. Plan adherence charts reveal whether planned volume is actually being hit week over week.

The planning layer makes training intentional: a weekly plan is built from templates and cardio targets, sessions flip to Done when logged and to Skipped with a reason when missed, and the AI can review the past week and propose a smarter next week — which the user approves one change at a time. When something feels off mid-week, one tap opens an adjustment flow: describe the constraint, review the proposed edits, commit.

Data flows in from Strava and Apple Health, so manually logged sessions sit alongside imported ones, and the AI has sleep, HRV, and resting HR as context when making recommendations. Everything stays in one place — no stitching, no copying, no switching apps.
